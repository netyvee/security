## Q1 — Dominance Assets: What is missing for London market dominance?

A dominant commercial cleaning or security company in London maintains a content library that serves as the primary reference resource for B2B procurement managers in their sector. Vigil currently has none of this.

### Missing dominance assets

**1. Resource Centre**
A `/resources/` or `/guides/` hub with:
- "The Complete Guide to Commercial Cleaning Contracts" (2,500 words)
- "TUPE Transition Checklist for Facilities Managers" (downloadable PDF)
- "Commercial Cleaning Specification Template" (editable Google Doc)
- "CQC Cleaning Compliance Checklist for Healthcare Managers"
- "CDM 2015 Welfare Facility Cleaning Standards Guide"
- "How to Evaluate a Commercial Cleaning Tender" (procurement framework)

For security:
- "SIA Licence Verification Guide for Procurement Managers"
- "Security Risk Assessment Template for Commercial Premises"
- "Event Security Briefing Template"
- "Construction Site Security Checklist (CDM 2015)"

These resources serve two functions: (a) they capture email addresses from procurement managers who download them, creating a warm nurture list; (b) they earn backlinks from industry publications, HR/facilities blogs, and trade associations who reference the resources.

**2. Sector Hub Pages (both sites)**
Neither site has pages that speak to buyer sectors, only service types. A property management director researching cleaning providers does not search "property management cleaning London" — they search based on their sector problems: "cleaning for residential property portfolio London", "single invoice cleaning contract multiple sites", "TUPE cleaning contract change London".

Missing:
- Cleaning: `/for-property-managers/`, `/for-corporate-offices/`, `/for-healthcare-managers/`, `/for-construction-contractors/`
- Security: `/for-retail-businesses/`, `/for-event-organisers/`, `/for-construction-sites/`, `/for-healthcare-facilities/`

**3. Comparison Content**
Procurement decisions involve comparisons. A page like "Directly Employed vs Agency Cleaners: What's the Real Difference?" would rank for comparative queries and frame the decision in Vigil's favour. Currently no comparison content exists on either site.

**4. Industry Statistics**
Both sites make claims without industry data. A page citing real industry statistics (cleaning industry turnover, staff turnover rates in agency vs direct employment, CQC inspection failure rates related to cleaning standards) would:
- Establish Vigil as an informed industry voice
- Be cited by industry publications and AI engines
- Provide backlink anchor content

## Q6 — Competitor Structural Analysis

### Cleanology (cleanology.com) — Commercial Cleaning
- **Pages:** Estimated 200+ (service pages × sector × location matrix)
- **Architecture:** Service hub → Service type → Service × Sector → Location
- **Location strategy:** Individual pages for all 32 boroughs + key postcodes
- **Content hub:** Full resource section with guides, case studies, blog
- **Key differentiator on site:** "Carbon Neutral Cleaning" — sustainability as a competitive differentiator with a full page and certification badge
- **What Vigil can take:** Sector pages (they have "for offices", "for retail", "for education" etc.)

### MITIE Cleaning (mitie.com/cleaning) — Enterprise Facilities
- **Pages:** 500+ (enterprise scale, not directly comparable)
- **Architecture:** Service → Sector → Insight (thought leadership)
- **Location strategy:** National, not London-specific
- **Key differentiator:** Tech-driven (MyMitie portal, IoT cleaning sensors)
- **What Vigil can take:** The "proof of service" concept (Vigil's digital visit sign-off is equivalent, just not marketed at MITIE's scale)

### Corps Security (corps-security.com) — UK Security
- **Pages:** 100+ service and sector pages
- **Architecture:** Service → Sector → Case study → Insight
- **Location strategy:** Regional hubs, not borough-level
- **Key differentiator:** SIA Approved Contractor status prominently displayed
- **What Vigil can take:** Case study architecture (they have 15+ sector-specific case studies) — this is the trust gap Vigil needs to close

### Key structural gap vs all three competitors
Every competitor has a **case study library** with 5+ named client stories. None of Vigil's sites have a single case study. This is the single most actionable trust gap — not because case studies are complex to build, but because the sales/operational data to write them exists (Vigil has current clients with successful outcomes) and the format is proven.

## The Four Growth Bets (what would move the business most)

### Bet 1: Case Studies (both sites) — 4 weeks
Three sector-specific case studies per site, written with real clients (anonymised if necessary). These become the site's most powerful conversion assets. Every service page links to a relevant case study. Both sites go from "trust claims" to "trust evidence."

### Bet 2: Borough expansion (both sites) — 8 weeks
Complete all 32 boroughs on both sites. 22 missing boroughs × 2 sites = 44 pages. Borough Agent in CLAUDE.md can produce each in 1 session. Once all 32 boroughs are covered, Vigil appears in 100% of "commercial cleaning [borough]" and "security company [borough]" searches in London — currently appears in 28%.

### Bet 3: Review acquisition (both sites) — ongoing
Systematically request Google reviews from current clients at Day 30 and Day 90 (already in CLAUDE.md lead nurture plan). Target: 10+ Google reviews per site in 6 months. This enables AggregateRating schema and rich results, which improve CTR by 15–30%.

### Bet 4: Activate the content agent — immediate
The autonomous content agent is built and ready. The only blocker is `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY` in Vercel environment. Once active, the site produces 2,000+ word content weekly without owner time. Over 6 months this creates 26+ articles covering buyer questions neither Cleanology nor Corps Security has answered — creating a content moat.

## Overall Growth Assessment

The sites are well-architected for a company that has been building for 6 months. The infrastructure quality (qualification flow, autonomous agents, email routing, CRM integration) is significantly ahead of where a 6-month-old cleaning/security startup would normally be. The gap is operational activation: agents need keys, borough pages need building, case studies need client conversations, and the content pipeline needs to actually publish.

The business growth score for the combined digital presence is **38/100** — not because the work is bad, but because the gap between "built" and "operational" is wide, and "operational" is what drives leads.
