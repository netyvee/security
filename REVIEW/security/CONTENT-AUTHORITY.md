## Findings

### F1 — Door supervisors page is absent
**Impact: Critical | Confidence: HIGH**

"Door supervisor London" is a commercially significant keyword cluster. Door supervisors are an SIA-licensed role with specific demand from:
- Nightclubs and bars
- Event venues
- Corporate buildings
- Large retail premises

The absence of this page means Vigil cannot appear in Google results for this category. This is not a "nice to have" — it is a missing revenue vertical. The page should follow the same structure as `/event-security-london/` (the closest related service) with specific door supervisor FAQ content and SIA licensing context.

---

### F2 — No sector hub content
**Impact: Critical | Confidence: HIGH**

Security is purchased differently by different sectors. A pub chain buying door supervisors has different concerns than a construction contractor buying site security. Currently all content is service-type focused, not buyer-sector focused.

High-value missing sector pages:
- `/security-for-retail/` — theft prevention, uniformed presence, SIA requirement context
- `/security-for-events/` — licensing, crowd management, DPS relationships
- `/security-for-construction/` — CDM 2015, access control, theft statistics
- `/security-for-healthcare/` — lone worker, CQC environment, NHS framework
- `/security-for-education/` — safeguarding, out-of-hours patrols, DBS requirements
- `/security-for-residential-developments/` — keyholding, concierge, new build management

Each of these pages would rank for a different buyer-intent query cluster and draw a different type of lead.

---

### F4 — No case studies
**Impact: High | Confidence: HIGH**

Security buyers are risk-averse by nature. A case study format provides the social proof that testimonials cannot: specific situation, specific solution, measurable outcome. Examples that would convert:
- "How Vigil secured a 6-site retail portfolio across East London"
- "Construction site security during a 12-month build programme in Canary Wharf"
- "Event security for a 2,000-person corporate gala — deployment and debrief"

These pages would rank for sector + location queries and provide the final conversion evidence for procurement teams.

---

### F5 — No SIA licence verification tool or page
**Impact: Medium | Confidence: MEDIUM**

The SIA licence checker exists at sia.homeoffice.gov.uk. A page on the security site that explains how to verify an officer's licence, with a link to the official checker and an explanation of what Vigil's licence numbers look like, would:
- Rank for "how do I check SIA licence" queries
- Demonstrate transparency and build trust
- Be the kind of page that earns backlinks from industry publications

---

### F6 — No pricing or cost content
**Impact: Medium | Confidence: HIGH**

Security buyers routinely research costs before contacting providers. No page addresses:
- How security costs are structured (hourly rates, contract minimums)
- What affects the cost (sector, hours, number of officers, patrol frequency)
- Why directly employed officers cost what they do (quality vs agency)

A "Security guard costs London" guide would capture early-stage buyers before they contact a competitor.

---

### F7 — No accreditation/trust hub
**Impact: Medium | Confidence: HIGH**

Vigil's permitted trust claims (SIA-licensed, DBS-checked, directly employed, £10M liability) are scattered across pages. A `/security-accreditations-and-standards/` page would:
- Consolidate all trust claims with explanations
- Be the target of "are Vigil security SIA licensed?" voice queries
- Allow the entity schema to reference a verifiable credentials page
